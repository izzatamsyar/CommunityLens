    Community Rights Module

    Know Your Rights

    Whatever the impact score, every community member has these basic rights when a development project is proposed nearby.

    📄

    Right to receive information

    You can ask for clear, accessible details about the project — what it is, where it is, and how it may affect your area.

    🗳️

    Right to participate in consultation

    You can attend public briefings, town halls, or consultation sessions held about the project before decisions are finalised.

    📝

    Right to provide feedback

    You can submit your views, concerns, or suggestions through the proper channels, and expect them to be recorded.

    📢

    Right to raise community concerns

    You can raise issues on behalf of your household or community group — you don't need to be an expert to speak up.

    ❓

    Right to seek clarification from authorities

    You can ask the relevant agency or developer to explain anything that is unclear before the project proceeds.

    💡 These rights are general principles drawn from Social Impact Assessment practice. Always check the specific laws, guidelines, and procedures that apply in your area for full details.
